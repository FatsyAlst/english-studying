First used 1765 in chess; compound of Middle English *stale* ('standstill,' from Anglo-French *estale*, related to 'stall/stand') + *mate* ('checkmate'). Figurative sense ('deadlock') from 1885.

---
> [!tip] Notes
> - Chess: a draw (not a loss); often a defensive tactic to avoid losing.
> - Figurative: describes neutral/negative situations with no clear winner.
> - Distinguished from 'checkmate' (winner determined) and 'deadlock' (mutual inability to proceed).
