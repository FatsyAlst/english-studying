Mid-16th century, from Latin *praeposterus* 'reversed, absurd' (*prae* 'before' + *post(er)us* 'coming after'), originally 'with the order reversed, back-to-front' (like 'putting the cart before the horse'), then generalized to 'absurd, ridiculous.'

---
> [!tip] Notes
> - Strong, emphatic word; often used to reject something as totally absurd or beyond reason, not just 'a bit silly.'
> - Common in spoken reactions ('That's preposterous!').
> - Similar: absurd, ridiculous, ludicrous; opposite: reasonable, sensible, logical.
