Formed from prefix *mis-* ('wrong, badly') + *fit* ('to be suitable'). First used for clothes that did not fit properly (early 1800s), later extended to people who 'don't fit in' socially (late 19th century).

---
> [!tip] Notes
> - Often negative if used by others (labeling someone as not fitting in).
> - Can be used ironically or positively in self-description ('we're proud misfits').
> - For people: describes someone who doesn't belong socially or culturally.
> - For objects: describes items that don't fit properly in size, shape, or type.
