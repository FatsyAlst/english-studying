---
Verb 'boggle' from late 16th century Scots, probably from *bogle* 'hobgoblin, spectre' (Middle English *bugge* 'bugbear,' possibly Scandinavian). 'Mind-boggling' coined mid-20th century for 'astounding'.

---
> [!tip] Notes
> - Mostly in 'mind-boggling' (informal, emphatic for incredible).
> - Implies overwhelming scale/effect.
> - Positive/neutral for amazement.
> - Synonyms: astounding, staggering.
