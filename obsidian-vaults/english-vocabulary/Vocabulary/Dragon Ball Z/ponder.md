Middle English (originally 'appraise, judge worth'): From Old French *ponderer* 'consider,' from Latin *ponderare* 'weigh, reflect on,' literally 'to weigh,' from *pondus, ponder-* 'weight' (from PIE root *pen-* 'to draw, stretch'). Mental sense from late 14th century.

---
> [!tip] Notes
> - Formal/literary; often with prepositions on/over/about.
> - Differs from 'think' by implying prolonged, serious reflection.
> - Not used for quick thoughts.
> - Similar to 'contemplate/muse/ruminate' but less intense than 'brood.'
