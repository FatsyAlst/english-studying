When I decided to improve my English through reading, I made a classic mistake: **I picked a philosophy book about Stoicism** (think Marcus Aurelius, Seneca, Epictetus).

### Why Philosophy Books Are Hard for Language Learners

| Problem | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Archaic vocabulary** | Words like "hitherto", "thereof", "whereupon" |
| **Abstract concepts** | Discussing virtue, fate, tranquility in complex ways |
| **Formal/academic tone** | Long, complex sentence structures |
| **Rare words** | Vocabulary you'll rarely encounter in daily life |
| **Translation artifacts** | Ancient texts translated into overly formal English |

### The Realistic Approach

For effective vocabulary acquisition, aim for:
- **5-10 new words per chapter** (not 40+!)
- Words you'll actually use in conversations
- Modern, everyday English
- Topics you genuinely enjoy
